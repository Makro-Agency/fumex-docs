The project, in brief

What Fumex needed, and what we built to deliver it.

Fumex sells industrial fume-extraction and air-filtration systems — a technical, B2B catalogue spanning several machine series, a replacement filter for almost every model, and accessories. Buyers are engineers and facility managers who research by application (laser marking, welding, soldering…) and industry (automotive, electronics, medical…), often need a quote rather than a one-click checkout, and frequently aren't sure which machine fits their process. The site had to sell, educate, generate qualified leads, and scale — without a developer in the loop for everyday content.

Under the hood: the Quiz engine

The most custom thing we built — a guided product recommender your sales team can edit without code.

The requirement

Choosing the right extraction system depends on what you're capturing (particulate, fumes, gases), how hard the machine runs (duty cycle) and the airflow you need (CFM) — judgement most buyers don't have. Fumex needed something that asks a few plain questions and points the visitor to the right machine, sends "not sure" buyers straight to a human, and — crucially — can be retuned by the team in Shopify as the range changes, without touching code.

How we built it

One pop-up, rendered once and reusable on every page (it opens from any "Take Quiz" button, the #quiz link, or programmatically). The questions live in the theme section; the answers-to-product mapping lives in a Shopify metaobject called Product Recommendation — a simple table of "if the answers are X, recommend series Y and these products." That separation is the whole point: marketing/sales own the recommendations; the logic stays put.

1
You fill the decision matrixEach Product Recommendation record = one combination of answers → a Series name + recommended products.
2
The page bakes it inWhen a page loads, the theme reads every Product Recommendation record and writes them (plus the products) into the page as hidden data.
3
Visitor answers 4 questionsTheir picks are held in memory as they click through; Back / Continue move between steps.
4
It finds the matching recordThe quiz looks for the single record whose four answers all match the visitor's. First match wins.
5
Result, or a humanMatch → shows the recommended Series + product cards (Add to cart, or Request a quote). No match, or "Not sure" → "Talk to an Expert," with the answers pre-filled into the quote form.

The four questions

These are defined in the quiz section (editable in the Theme Editor). Question 4 is skipped automatically if the visitor says they don't know their CFM, and every question has a "Not sure" option that politely ends the quiz and routes to support.

#QuestionOptions
1Which best describes your filtration need?Particulate matter · Fumes · Gases · Mix of all three · Not sure
2What is the duty cycle?Intermittent · Continuous · Not sure
3Do you know your required air flow (CFM)?Yes · No · Not sure
4What is your required air flow (CFM)? (skipped if Q3 = No)0–200 · 201–300 · 301–500 · 500–900 · Not sure
⚠️
The one rule that keeps it working: the answer wording in a question must exactly match the value typed into the matching Product Recommendation record (capitalisation aside). If you rename an option to "Dust & particles" but the record still says "Particulate matter," that path will stop matching and fall through to "Talk to an Expert." Change them together.

Changing it yourself

Change a recommendation

Edit a record in Content → Metaobjects → Product Recommendation: set the four answer values and the products/series it should return.

No code

Reword a question or option

Edit the quiz section in the Theme Editor — then update the matching records so the wording still lines up.

Theme + metaobject

Add a product to a result

Open the relevant Product Recommendation record and add it to Recommended products. Products tagged request-quote show a quote button instead of Add to cart.

No code
💡 Nice touch: when the quiz hands off to the contact form, it passes the visitor's answers and the recommended series along in the link, so the HubSpot quote form arrives pre-filled — less typing for the buyer, better-qualified leads for sales.
