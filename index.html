Your Fumex Website — Owner's Guide

A plain-English guide to everything we built: every page, every feature, and exactly where to go in Shopify to change things yourself. No code required.

Theme: Makro Core Platform: Shopify Built by Makro Agency Updated June 2026

Start here

How to read this guide and the one rule that keeps your site safe.

Your website runs on Shopify — the same platform that powers millions of online stores. We built you a custom theme called Makro Core, designed specifically for Fumex's products, applications and industries.

This guide walks through your site page by page. For each one you'll see:

  • What it is — what the page does and what's on it.
  • How it's built — the building blocks ("sections") that make it up.
  • How to edit it — the exact place in your Shopify admin to make changes.

💡 The golden rule

Editing content (text, images, products, prices, blog posts) is always safe — go ahead. Editing code (anything under Online Store → Themes → Edit code) can break the design. When in doubt, change content in the visual editor, and leave code to Makro.

Finding your way in Shopify

The three places you'll spend all your time.

Everything you'll ever edit lives in one of three areas of your Shopify admin (admin.shopify.com):

1. The Theme Editor — the look of your pages

This is the drag-and-drop visual editor where you change what appears on each page: banners, text, images, the order of things, colours and fonts.

📍 Where to find it

Online Store → Themes → Customize (on the Makro Core theme). A live preview opens with an editing panel on the left. Use the page dropdown at the very top to switch between Homepage, Product pages, Collections, etc.

2. Content areas — your products, pages, blogs and menus

The actual "stuff" on your site — products and their prices, written pages, blog articles, navigation menus, and the special Applications / Industries / OEM content (called metaobjects).

📍 Where to find it

The left sidebar of your admin: Products, Content → Pages, Content → Blog posts, Content → Metaobjects, and Content → Menus (for navigation).

3. Theme Settings — site-wide design

Global choices that apply everywhere: brand colours, fonts, logo, social links, the cart style, free-shipping message, and so on. Covered in Colours & fonts.

✏️ A safe habit

The theme editor saves only when you press Save (top right). Nothing goes live until you do. To experiment safely, ask Makro to set up a duplicate of the theme to play in.

Header & navigation

The bar at the top of every page.

Your header stays fixed to the top as visitors scroll. From left to right it has: the Fumex logo, the main menu (Products, Solutions, Help Center, Blog, About, Contact), a search icon, a cart icon, and the yellow Request Quote button.

The first four menu items open mega-menus — large dropdown panels with a heading and links — so visitors can jump straight to the right section.

📍 Where to find it

Theme Editor → Customize, then click the header at the top of the preview. The links themselves come from Content → Menus (the menu named Main menu and its sub-menus).

✏️ To change what's in the menu

Go to Content → Menus, open Main menu, and add/remove/reorder links. To change the dropdown headings or the Request Quote button, use the header settings in the Theme Editor.

Footer

The dark-blue band at the bottom of every page: a "Have Questions? Contact Us" prompt, a newsletter sign-up, and five columns of links (Products, Solutions, Help Center, About, Blog), plus the copyright line.

✏️ How to edit it

Each column of links is a menu under Content → Menus (products, solutions, help-center, about, blog). The headings, newsletter text and colours are in the footer settings of the Theme Editor.

Product collections

The pages that list your products: All Products, Filters and Accessories.

A collection is a group of products. Your main All Products page is special: products are organised into tabbed groups (for example F-Series and G-Series units, then replacement filter sets), so customers can find the right line quickly.

All Products

/collections/all-products
✏️ To add or remove products

Go to Products in your admin to create or edit a product, then Products → Collections to control which collection it appears in. Products are sorted into the F/G tabs by their tags — keep using the same tag names so new products land in the right group.

📍 To change the page layout

Theme Editor → page dropdown → Collection. The grouped grid is a section called Custom product grid.

Product page

Where a customer reads about one product and adds it to cart.

Product detail

/products/…

Each product page shows the image gallery, title, price, options, an Add to cart / buy area, plus a Specifications table, a Replacement Filters section, and the Quiz/Support panels.

There are three product layouts depending on the product type:

  • Standard product — most products.
  • Filter product — adds a product video and filtration context.

Go to Products, open the product, and edit its title, description, images, price and variants there. The Specifications and Replacement Filters come from the product's metafields (extra info fields) — edit those further down the same product page.

📍 To change the layout for all products

Theme Editor → page dropdown → Products → Default product (or Filter / Accessories for the other two layouts).

This is one of the smartest parts of your site. There's an Applications hub that lists every application, and each application has its own detail page. The detail pages are metaobject-driven — meaning you fill in a simple content record and the page builds itself. Add a new application record and a new page appears automatically, perfectly styled.

The content engine

How your Applications, Industries and OEM pages build themselves — the part worth understanding.

Rather than hand-building dozens of near-identical pages, we use metaobjects. Think of a metaobject as a fill-in-the-blanks form: you complete the fields (title, image, description, recommended products), press save, and a fully designed page appears at its own web address. Edit the record and the page updates. It keeps every application/industry page perfectly consistent.

MetaobjectPowersWhere to manage
Individual Application PagesEvery application detail pageContent → Metaobjects
Individual Industry PagesEvery industry detail pageContent → Metaobjects
OEMThe OEM programme page(s)Content → Metaobjects
Product RecommendationThe answers behind the Product Selector QuizContent → Metaobjects
✏️ Adding a new application page — step by step

1. Go to Content → Metaobjects → Individual Application Pages.
2. Click Add entry.
3. Fill in the fields (title, hero image, description, recommended products).
4. Press Save. Your new page is live at /pages/individual-application/your-title.
5. (Optional) Add a card linking to it on the homepage and the Applications hub.

⚠️ Keep the fields filled

Because the page design relies on these fields, leaving an image or heading blank can leave a gap on the page. Fill every field that the existing entries use.

Colours & fonts

The site-wide design settings.

Your brand styling is controlled centrally so the whole site stays consistent:

  • Fonts — Headings & body in DM Sans; labels, buttons and menus in Roboto Mono (the uppercase, technical-looking text).
  • Colours — yellow primary buttons (#F7D400), blue accents (#2e69fd), dark backgrounds, blue badges.
  • Shape — rounded corners (10px) and fully-rounded (pill) buttons.
  • Logo, favicon, social links, dark-mode — all set here too.
📍 Where to find it

Theme Editor → Theme settings (the gear icon at the bottom of the left panel) → Colors, Typography, Logo, etc.

⚠️ Go gently

These settings affect every page at once. Change one thing at a time and preview before saving.

How we built it

A short, non-technical summary of the approach — for the curious.

We built Fumex a custom Shopify theme ("Makro Core") from the ground up rather than using an off-the-shelf template. The design is assembled from reusable sections (banners, grids, sliders) so pages can be rearranged in the visual editor without touching code.

The clever part is the content architecture. Fumex has many applications, many industries and several product lines. Instead of building and maintaining each page by hand, we created metaobjects — structured content records that automatically generate consistent, on-brand pages. Add a record, get a page. This means the site scales as Fumex grows, with no developer needed for new applications or industries.

We also connected the tools your team already uses — HubSpot for leads, Gorgias for support, ReCharge for subscriptions — so everything works together, and built in conversion features like the Product Selector Quiz to guide visitors to the right system.
